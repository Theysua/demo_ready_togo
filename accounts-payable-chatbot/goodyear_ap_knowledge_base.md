# Goodyear Accounts Payable - Global Policy & Procedures

## 1. Overview
This document outlines the standard operating procedures and policies for the Accounts Payable (AP) process at Goodyear. This knowledge base serves as the single source of truth for both internal Goodyear employees and external vendors regarding invoice submission, approval workflows, payment terms, and exception handling.

## 2. Invoice Submission Guidelines (Vendor Guide)
To ensure timely processing and payment, all vendors must adhere to the following invoice submission requirements:

### Accepted Formats
- Invoices must be submitted in **PDF format** only.
- Each PDF file must contain only ONE invoice (including any supporting documentation as subsequent pages).
- Maximum file size per submission is 10MB.

### Required Information on Invoices
Every invoice submitted to Goodyear must clearly state:
1. **Valid Goodyear Purchase Order (PO) Number**: Missing or incorrect PO numbers will result in automatic rejection.
2. **Vendor Information**: Correct Legal Name, Address, and Goodyear Vendor ID (if known).
3. **Goodyear "Bill To" Entity**: The specific Goodyear corporate entity requesting the goods/services.
4. **Invoice Details**: Unique Invoice Number, Invoice Date, and Currency.
5. **Line Item Details**: Description of goods/services matching the PO, Quantity, Unit Price, and Total Amount.
6. **Tax Information**: Applicable taxes must be broken out clearly.

### Submission Channels
- **Primary Method**: Submit via the Goodyear Vendor Portal (preferred).
- **Secondary Method**: Email to the designated regional AP inbox (e.g., `ap_invoices_na@goodyear.com` for North America).

## 3. Internal Approval Workflow
All non-PO invoices and PO invoices with discrepancies require internal approval before payment can be released.

### Standard PO Invoices (3-Way Match)
For standard inventory and service POs, Goodyear utilizes a 3-way matching process:
1. **Purchase Order (PO)**: Approved and issued to vendor.
2. **Goods Receipt (GR) / Service Entry Sheet (SES)**: Confirmed by the business requester in SAP upon receipt of goods or completion of services.
3. **Invoice (INV)**: Received from vendor.

*If PO, GR, and INV match within tolerance levels, the invoice is automatically approved and scheduled for payment. No manual approval is required.*

### Discrepancies and Exceptions
If a 3-way match fails, the following workflows apply:
- **Missing Goods Receipt (GR)**: The business requester will receive a notification to verify receipt and process the GR in SAP. The invoice will remain in "Blocked" or "Pending GR" status.
- **Price/Quantity Variance**: If the invoice exceeds the PO amount by more than the accepted tolerance (generally 5% or $50, whichever is lower), it is routed to the PO Creator/Buyer for review and resolution.

### Non-PO Invoices
Invoices submitted without a PO (where permitted by policy) must follow a hierarchical approval chain based on the Total Amount and the Cost Center/Department rules.

## 4. Standard Payment Terms
Unless otherwise specified in a specific vendor contract, Goodyear's standard global payment terms are **Net 60 days** from the invoice receipt date.
- **Early Payment Discounts**: Goodyear occasionally offers early payment programs (e.g., 2% Net 10). Vendors must enroll via the Procurement portal to participate.
- **Payment Runs**: Standard payment runs occur twice weekly (Tuesdays and Thursdays).

## 5. Escalation Matrix
For unresolved AP issues, follow the escalation path below:
- **Level 1**: Chatbot & AP Self-Service Portal (Status checks, basic policy queries).
- **Level 2**: Regional AP Helpdesk (Ticket submission for complex discrepancies).
- **Level 3**: AP Team Lead (Escalation for critical vendor holds or urgent payments).

## 6. Supplier Master Data Updates
Vendors needing to update their master data (e.g., bank account changes, address changes, name changes) must NOT submit these requests via the AP invoice channel.
- All master data updates must be initiated through the **Goodyear Supplier Lifecycle Management (SLM) Portal**.
- Bank changes require secondary verification (e.g., phone call confirmation) to prevent fraud.
